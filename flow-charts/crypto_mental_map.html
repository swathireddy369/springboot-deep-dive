ü§ù Diffie-Hellman

What it is:

Key exchange method. Two parties agree on shared secret over insecure channel‚Äîwithout ever transmitting the key.

How it works:

1. Agree on public numbers
2. Each picks private number + does math
3. Swap results, do final math
4. Both arrive at same secret!

Why it matters:

Underpins TLS/HTTPS handshake. How browsers and servers establish secure channels.
